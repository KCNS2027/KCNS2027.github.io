Malcom X: Cory United Methodist Church Cleveland, Ohio

The Cory Methodist Church in Ohio holds a special place in the history of Malcolm X, as it was the site of one of his most famous speeches - the "Ballot or the Bullet" speech. This speech was a rallying cry for black people to take political action and demand their rights as citizens of the United States. Malcolm X gave the speech on April 3, 1964, to a packed audience at the church. In the speech, he urged black people to use their voting power to bring about change and fight against racism and inequality. The speech is considered a pivotal moment in the civil rights movement and helped to inspire a new generation of activists. Today, the Cory Methodist Church is recognized as a historic landmark and a symbol of the struggle for equality and justice.
